## 🏅 Overview

Each participant is scored out of **30 points** across 3 criteria. Judges observe both the **prompting process** and the **2-minute presentation**.

---

## 📊 Criteria 1 — Prompt Strategy (0–10 pts)

> *Did the participant break the problem into smart, logical steps?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **9–10** | Exceptional — Prompts are structured, specific, and build on each other logically. Each prompt has a clear purpose. |
| **7–8** | Good — Mostly logical sequence with minor gaps. At least 4 quality prompts used. |
| **5–6** | Average — Some structure, but prompts are too vague or repetitive. |
| **3–4** | Weak — Jumped to conclusions, asked broad questions, little strategy visible. |
| **0–2** | Poor — Single prompt used or prompts were irrelevant to the case. |

### ✅ What to Look For
- Did they start broad and get more specific?
- Did they use follow-up prompts based on AI responses?
- Did they ask the AI to simulate, role-play, or analyse?
- Did they avoid asking for the direct answer in one shot?

---

## 📊 Criteria 2 — Investigation Depth (0–10 pts)

> *How deeply did they explore the problem using AI?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **9–10** | Explored multiple angles — causes, evidence, simulations, comparisons. |
| **7–8** | Covered 2–3 different angles with meaningful AI outputs shown. |
| **5–6** | Stayed mostly surface-level. AI responses were generic. |
| **3–4** | Explored only one angle. Investigation felt incomplete. |
| **0–2** | Barely investigated. Used AI as a search engine, not a tool. |

### ✅ What to Look For
- Did they explore **causes**, **evidence**, and **solutions** separately?
- Did they use AI creatively (simulate customer complaints, write reports, compare scenarios)?
- Were the AI outputs specific and insightful or vague and generic?

---

## 📊 Criteria 3 — Final Verdict (0–10 pts)

> *How clear, confident, and well-supported is their conclusion?*

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **9–10** | Clear 3-point verdict with strong justification. Presented confidently and concisely. |
| **7–8** | Good conclusion with 2–3 points. Mostly clear delivery. |
| **5–6** | Verdict exists but is vague or poorly justified. |
| **3–4** | Conclusion is weak, confused, or unsupported by their investigation. |
| **0–2** | No clear verdict given. |

### ✅ What to Look For
- Did they state **specific root causes** (not just "bad management")?
- Did they connect their verdict back to the **AI evidence** they found?
- Was the presentation **structured** (problem → journey → verdict)?
- Did they speak with **confidence** in their 2 minutes?

## 🧮 Scoring Summary Table

| Criteria | Max | Focus |
|----------|-----|-------|
| Prompt Strategy | /10 | Quality & logic of prompts used |
| Investigation Depth | /10 | How thoroughly they explored |
| Final Verdict | /10 | Clarity and strength of conclusion |
| **Total** | **/30** | |

---

## 👥 Judge Panel Recommendation

- **3 judges** recommended (2 faculty + 1 senior student or industry guest)
- Each judge scores independently
- Final score = **average of 3 judges** per criteria
- In case of a tie → judges discuss and re-score on Verdict only
