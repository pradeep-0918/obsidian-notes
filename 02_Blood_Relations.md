## 🧒 What are Blood Relations? (Feynman Explanation)

Think about your own family. You have a **mom, dad, maybe a brother or sister, grandparents, uncles, aunts, and cousins**. All of these people are your **relatives** — connected to you by blood (or marriage).

In aptitude, someone gives you a **confusing sentence** like:
> *"Pointing to a girl, Ram said, 'She is the daughter of my grandfather's only son.'"*

And you have to figure out: **Who is she to Ram?**

The secret? **Always draw a family tree!** 🌳

---

## 🌳 Family Tree — The Golden Tool

```
                    Grandfather (GF) — Grandmother (GM)
                           |
              ┌────────────┴────────────┐
           Father (F)              Uncle/Aunt
              |
         ┌────┴────┐
       YOU        Brother/Sister
              |
           Son/Daughter
```

### ⚡ 1-Second Rule: ALWAYS draw this tree. Never solve in your head!

## 👨‍👩‍👧 Key Relationship Words (Memorize These!)

### Blood Relations
| Relation | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Father's father | Grandfather (Paternal) |
| Mother's father | Grandfather (Maternal) |
| Father's brother | Uncle (Paternal) |
| Mother's brother | Maternal Uncle |
| Father's sister | Aunt (Paternal) |
| Brother's son | Nephew |
| Brother's daughter | Niece |
| Sister's son | Nephew |
| Sister's daughter | Niece |
| Son's wife | Daughter-in-law |
| Daughter's husband | Son-in-law |
| Brother's wife | Sister-in-law |
| Wife's/Husband's brother | Brother-in-law |

## 🟢 BASIC LEVEL

### Type 1 — Simple Single-Step Relation

#### Example 1:
> "Ravi is the son of Priya." How is Priya related to Ravi?

**Solution:**
```
Ravi ← son of ← Priya
So Priya is Ravi's MOTHER ✅
```

#### Example 2:
> "A is the brother of B. B is the mother of C. How is A related to C?"

**Tree:**
```
      A (Male) — B (Female)
      siblings
      B is mother of C
      So A is uncle of C ✅
```

#### ⚡ Trick: Use symbols!
- `+` = Male
- `-` = Female
- `→` = child of
- `=` = married to

---

### Type 2 — Two-Step Relations

#### Example:
> "X's mother is Y's daughter." How is X related to Y?

**Tree:**
```
Y
└── Y's daughter (X's mother)
    └── X

Y is X's mother's mother = X's GRANDMOTHER ✅
```

## 🟡 INTERMEDIATE LEVEL

### Type 3 — Pointing/Photograph Problems

**The Format:** "Pointing to a photo, A says..."

#### Example:
> Pointing to a photo, Neha says, "He is the son of my father's only son."
> Who is in the photo?

**Solution:**
```
Neha's father's only son = Neha's brother (if she has one)
                         OR Neha's father himself (if he's the only son)

"My father's only son" = Neha's BROTHER (or Neha herself if she's only child)

The photo shows brother's son = Neha's NEPHEW ✅
```

#### ⚡ Trick: "Father's only son" = The person themselves (if no other siblings mentioned)!

---

### Type 4 — coded Relations

#### Example:
> A + B means A is father of B
> A − B means A is mother of B  
> A × B means A is brother of B
> A ÷ B means A is sister of B
>
> What does P + Q − R × S mean?

**Solution (read left to right):**
```
P + Q → P is father of Q
Q − R → Q is mother of R (so Q is female)
R × S → R is brother of S

P is father of Q → Q is P's child
Q is mother of R → R is Q's child (P's grandchild)
R is brother of S → S is also P's grandchild

P is GRANDFATHER of S ✅
```

---

### Type 5 — Puzzle Type (Multiple Clues)

#### Example:
```
1. A is the father of B
2. C is the daughter of A
3. D is the brother of E
4. E is the daughter of B
5. How is D related to A?
```

**Draw the tree:**
```
A (male)
├── B (male — A's son)
│   └── E (female — B's daughter) ← D's sister
│       └── D (male — brother of E)
└── C (female — A's daughter)

D is B's son → D is A's GRANDSON ✅
```

## 🔴 ADVANCED LEVEL

### Type 6 — Family Puzzle (6+ Clues)

These appear in big exams. Strategy:

```
Step 1: List all people mentioned
Step 2: Note gender (male/female) of each
Step 3: Draw the tree clue by clue
Step 4: Answer the question from the tree
```

#### Example:
```
- P and Q are a married couple
- R is the brother of Q
- S is the daughter of P
- T is the brother of S
- U is the mother of R
```
> How is U related to T?

**Tree:**
```
U
└── R (U's son) — Q (Q is R's sister, so Q is also U's child!)

P = Q (married) → P is Q's spouse
P and Q have: S (daughter), T (son — brother of S)

T is P and Q's son
Q is U's daughter
So T is U's GRANDSON ✅
```

---

### Type 7 — Generation Problems

#### Example:
> A is 3 generations below B. B is the grandfather of C. How many generations is A below C?

```
B
└── C's parent
    └── C ← B is 2 generations above C

A is 3 generations below B
C is 2 generations below B
A is (3-2) = 1 generation below C

A is C's CHILD/NEPHEW (1 gen below) ✅
```

## ⚡ MASTER SHORTCUT TRICKS

### Trick 1: Gender Identification Words
| Male Words | Female Words |
|------------|-------------|
| Father, Son, Brother, Uncle, Nephew, Grandfather, Husband, Grandson | Mother, Daughter, Sister, Aunt, Niece, Grandmother, Wife, Granddaughter |

### Trick 2: "Only Son / Only Child" Trap
> "Father's only son" = **The speaker themselves!**

### Trick 3: "In-Law" Rule
- Add "in-law" to the base relation when connected through marriage
- Son's wife = Daughter-in-**law**
- Brother's wife = Sister-in-**law**

### Trick 4: Two-Generation Jump Formula
```
Son's son = Grandson
Daughter's daughter = Granddaughter
Father's father = Grandfather
Mother's mother = Grandmother
```

## 🧪 Practice Questions

### 🟢 Basic
1. "A is the brother of B. B is the father of C." How is A related to C?
2. "X's sister is Y's mother." How is X related to Y?

### 🟡 Intermediate
3. Pointing to a boy, a woman says, "He is the son of my grandfather's only son." How is the boy related to the woman?
4. A × B means A is mother of B. A + B means A is sister of B. What does P × Q + R mean?

### 🔴 Advanced
5. In a family: P is the husband of Q. R is the brother of P. S is the son of R. T is the daughter of Q. U is the wife of S. How is T related to U?

---

## 📝 Answers

1. A is C's **Uncle**
2. X is Y's **Uncle/Aunt** (X's sister = Y's mother, so X is sibling of Y's mother)
3. Grandfather's only son = Woman's father → Boy is son of father = **Brother**
4. P is mother of Q, Q is sister of R → P is **Mother** of R (P × Q + R → P is mother of R)
5. T is Q's daughter, P is Q's husband (T's father), R is P's brother, S is R's son, U is S's wife → T and S are cousins → U is T's **Cousin's wife (Sister-in-law by extension)**
