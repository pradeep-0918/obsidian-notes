## 🍎 Feynman Explanation (Like You're 6)

Imagine your mom is **30 years old** and you are **10 years old**.

Someone asks: *"How old will your mom be when you are 20?"*

You think: *"I'm getting 10 years older... so mom also gets 10 years older → 30 + 10 = **40**!"*

**That's an ages problem.** Simple, right? 🎉

---

## 🔑 The Big Secret

> **The difference between two people's ages NEVER changes.**

If Mom is **20 years older** than you today → she will ALWAYS be 20 years older.
- Today: You = 10, Mom = 30 → difference = 20 ✅
- In 5 years: You = 15, Mom = 35 → difference = 20 ✅
- In 50 years: You = 60, Mom = 80 → difference = 20 ✅

🔥 **Memorize this. It's the #1 trick.**

## 🏗️ Types of Ages Problems

| Type | Example Trigger Words |
|------|-----------------------|
| **Present Age** | "How old is X now?" |
| **Past Age** | "X years ago, age was..." |
| **Future Age** | "X years later / after / hence..." |
| **Ratio** | "Ages are in ratio 2:3" |
| **Sum/Difference** | "Sum of ages is 50" |
| **Combo** | Mix of the above |

---

## 🧠 How to READ an Ages Problem

### 3-Step Reading Method:

**Step 1 → Find WHO** (how many people?)
**Step 2 → Find WHEN** (now? past? future?)
**Step 3 → Find WHAT** (what are you asked to find?)

### Example:
> *"5 years ago, Ram was 3 times as old as Shyam. Find their present ages if Ram is 10 years older than Shyam."*

- **WHO** → Ram and Shyam (2 people)
- **WHEN** → "5 years ago" = past
- **WHAT** → Find present ages

## 📌 Key Vocabulary Cheatsheet

| Word/Phrase | Math Meaning |
|-------------|--------------|
| "X years ago" | **Subtract X** from present age |
| "X years hence / later / after" | **Add X** to present age |
| "Twice as old" | **Multiply by 2** |
| "Thrice as old" | **Multiply by 3** |
| "Half as old" | **Divide by 2** |
| "Sum of ages" | **Add** both ages |
| "Difference of ages" | **Subtract** smaller from larger |
| "Ages in ratio a:b" | Ages = **ak and bk** |
