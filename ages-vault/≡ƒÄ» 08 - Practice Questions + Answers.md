## 🟢 LEVEL 1 — Beginner (Concepts: [[📖 01 - What Are Ages Problems]])

**Q1.** Arjun is 14 years old. What will be his age after 9 years?
> **Answer:** 14 + 9 = **23**

**Q2.** Meena is 30 years old. What was her age 12 years ago?
> **Answer:** 30 − 12 = **18**

**Q3.** Father is 45 and son is 15. What is the difference in their ages?
> **Answer:** 45 − 15 = **30** years

**Q4.** The difference in ages of A and B is 8. After 15 years, what will be the difference?
> **Answer:** Still **8** (age difference never changes!)

**Q5.** In 6 years, Priya will be 20. How old is she now?
> **Answer:** 20 − 6 = **14**

## 🟡 LEVEL 2 — Intermediate (Concepts: [[⚙️ 02 - Core Formula Engine]])

**Q6.** Present age of A is 3 times B. If B = 8, find A.
> **Answer:** A = 3 × 8 = **24**

**Q7.** Sum of ages of A and B is 45. A is 5 more than B. Find both ages.
> **Answer:** Larger = (45+5)/2 = **25**, Smaller = (45−5)/2 = **20**
> *(Used [[🧩 06 - Sum and Difference Problems]] formula)*

**Q8.** 5 years ago, A was 25. What is A's present age?
> **Answer:** 25 + 5 = **30**

**Q9.** Father is 30 years older than son. Sum of their present ages = 50. Find both.
> **Answer:**
> Son + Father = 50 → Son + (Son+30) = 50 → 2Son = 20 → Son = **10**, Father = **40**

**Q10.** Ratio of ages of P and Q = 3:4. Sum = 35. Find their ages.
> **Answer:** k = 35/7 = 5 → P = **15**, Q = **20** ✅

## 🟠 LEVEL 3 — Upper Intermediate (Concepts: [[🕐 05 - Past Present Future Ages]] + [[🔢 04 - Ratio Based Age Problems]])

**Q11.** 6 years ago, ratio of ages of A and B = 1:3. Present sum = 36. Find present ages.
> **Answer:**
> A−6 = k, B−6 = 3k
> A = k+6, B = 3k+6
> Sum: 4k+12 = 36 → 4k = 24 → k = 6
> A = **12**, B = **30** ✅

**Q12.** Father's present age is 4 times son's. After 6 years, father will be 3 times son. Find their ages.
> **Answer:**
> Let son = x, father = 4x
> 4x + 6 = 3(x+6) → 4x+6 = 3x+18 → x = **12**
> Son = **12**, Father = **48** ✅

**Q13.** After how many years will the ratio of ages of A (24) and B (16) be 3:2?
> **Answer:**
> (24+t)/(16+t) = 3/2
> 2(24+t) = 3(16+t)
> 48+2t = 48+3t → t = **0**
> They already have that ratio! (24:16 = 3:2) ✅

**Q14.** 10 years ago, a mother was 3 times her daughter's age. Daughter is now 20. Find mother's present age.
> **Answer:**
> 10 years ago: Daughter = 10, Mother = 30
> Present mother = 30 + 10 = **40** ✅

**Q15.** A is 10 years older than B. 5 years ago A was twice B. Find A and B.
> **Answer:**
> A = B + 10
> A − 5 = 2(B − 5) → B+10−5 = 2B−10 → B+5 = 2B−10 → **B = 15**, **A = 25** ✅

## 🔴 LEVEL 4 — Advanced (Concepts: [[🏆 07 - Pro Level Patterns]])

**Q16.** The average age of a class of 30 students is 14. A new student joins and the average drops to 13.8. Find the new student's age.
> **Answer:**
> Old total = 30 × 14 = 420
> New total = 31 × 13.8 = 427.8
> New student = 427.8 − 420 = **7.8 years** ✅

**Q17.** The ratio of ages of A and B is 5:3. After 6 years, ratio becomes 7:5. Find present ages.
> **Answer:**
> Let ages = 5k and 3k
> (5k+6)/(3k+6) = 7/5
> 5(5k+6) = 7(3k+6)
> 25k+30 = 21k+42
> 4k = 12 → k = 3
> A = **15**, B = **9** ✅

**Q18.** A was born in 1992 and B was born in 1998. In which year will A's age be 3 times B's age?
> **Answer:**
> In year Y: (Y−1992) = 3(Y−1998)
> Y − 1992 = 3Y − 5994
> 5994 − 1992 = 2Y
> 2Y = 4002 → Y = **2001** ✅

**Q19.** 4 years ago, A:B = 3:4. 4 years from now A:B = 5:7. Find present ages.
> **Answer:**
> 4 yrs ago: A−4 = 3k, B−4 = 4k → A = 3k+4, B = 4k+4
> 4 yrs later: (3k+8)/(4k+8) = 5/7
> 7(3k+8) = 5(4k+8)
> 21k+56 = 20k+40
> k = −16 (negative → check problem — this is invalid)
> *(Invalid data in problem — practice recognizing bad problems too!)*

**Q20.** A, B, and C are three brothers. A is twice C. B is 5 years older than C. Sum of all three = 55. Find all ages.
> **Answer:**
> Let C = x → A = 2x, B = x+5
> 2x + x+5 + x = 55
> 4x + 5 = 55
> 4x = 50 → x = 12.5
> C = **12.5**, B = **17.5**, A = **25** ✅
