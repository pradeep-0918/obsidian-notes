## 🕰️ The Timeline — Your Best Friend

> Draw a timeline for EVERY problem. It takes 5 seconds and saves you from silly mistakes.

```
PAST ←─────────────────────────────────→ FUTURE

 [n years ago]    [NOW]    [n years later]
      ●─────────────●─────────────●
   (x − n)         (x)         (x + n)
```

**Rule:** Move LEFT = Subtract | Move RIGHT = Add

---

## 📌 TYPE 1 — One Person, Two Time Points

**Template:**
> "A's age now is X. What was/will be A's age n years ago/later?"

This is the simplest type.

| Time | Formula |
|------|---------|
| n years ago | X − n |
| n years later | X + n |

---

## 📌 TYPE 2 — One Condition in Past, Find Present

**Template:**
> "n years ago, A's age was Y. Find present age."

→ Present age = Y + n

**Example:** 7 years ago, Priya was 13. Present age = 13 + 7 = **20** ✅

---

## 📌 TYPE 3 — Two People, Condition in Past

**Template:**
> "n years ago, A was k times B. Find present ages."

**Method:**
1. Let present age of B = x
2. Present age of A = (find from another condition, or express)
3. n years ago: B = x−n, A = (A's present) − n
4. Set up: A − n = k(x − n)
5. Solve

**Example:**
> 8 years ago, father was 5 times his son. Son is currently 18. Find father's present age.

Let father's present age = F

8 years ago: father = F−8, son = 18−8 = 10

Condition: F − 8 = 5 × 10
→ F − 8 = 50
→ **F = 58** ✅

⏱️ **Solved in 5 seconds** using shortcut from [[⚡ 03 - Shortcut Tricks Arsenal]]!

---

## 📌 TYPE 4 — Two Conditions (Present + Future/Past)

**Template:**
> Two equations involving the same two unknowns → simultaneous equations

**Example:**
> "A is 4 years older than B. After 6 years, A will be twice B."

Equations:
1. A = B + 4
2. A + 6 = 2(B + 6)

From (2): B + 4 + 6 = 2B + 12
→ B + 10 = 2B + 12
→ **B = −2** ← Negative! Check the problem.

*(This example is invalid — negative ages mean the problem has wrong data. Always verify!)*

Valid Example:
> "A is 20 years older than B. 5 years ago, A was 3 times B."

Equations:
1. A = B + 20
2. A − 5 = 3(B − 5)

Substitute (1) into (2):
(B + 20) − 5 = 3B − 15
B + 15 = 3B − 15
30 = 2B
**B = 15**, **A = 35** ✅

---

## 📌 TYPE 5 — Three Time Points in One Problem

**Example:**
> "10 years ago, A was X. 5 years later from now, A will be Y. Find present age."

Draw timeline:
```
[10 yrs ago]    [NOW]    [5 yrs later]
     X       →   ?    →      Y
```

Check: X + 10 = present age
Also: Y − 5 = present age

Both must give same answer. If not, the problem gives both as clues to find something else.

## 🚦 SIGNAL WORDS → ACTION TABLE

| Signal Word | What to Do |
|-------------|------------|
| "ago", "before", "back" | **Subtract** that many years |
| "hence", "later", "after", "from now" | **Add** that many years |
| "currently", "now", "at present" | **No change** — use x directly |
| "was", "were" (past tense) | Past → **subtract** |
| "will be", "shall be" | Future → **add** |

---

## 🔁 The "Work Backwards" Trick

> Sometimes it's faster to start from the FUTURE or PAST age and work to present.

**Example:**
> "In 10 years, Ram will be 35. How old is he now?"

Work backwards: 35 − 10 = **25** ← 1 second!
