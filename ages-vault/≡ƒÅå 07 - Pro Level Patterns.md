## 🔴 PATTERN 1 — The Classic Father-Son

**Signature:** "Father is X times son. After Y years, Z times."
**Speed Method:** [[⚡ 03 - Shortcut Tricks Arsenal]] — Trick 2 (Multiple Flip)

**Key shortcut:**
Let son = s → father = ks
- After n years: (ks+n)/(s+n) = new ratio
- Cross multiply → solve

**Typical answer trap:** Always verify age > 0!

---

## 🔴 PATTERN 2 — The "How Many Years Ago/Hence" Problem

**Signature:** "After how many years will ratio of ages be X:Y?"
**Speed Method:** Trick 5 from [[⚡ 03 - Shortcut Tricks Arsenal]]

**Template:**
```
(A + t)/(B + t) = p/q
Cross multiply, isolate t
t = (qA − pB)/(p − q)
```

**Example:**
Present ages: A=24, B=16. After how many years will ratio be 5:4?

t = (4×24 − 5×16)/(5−4) = (96−80)/1 = **16 years** ✅

---

## 🔴 PATTERN 3 — The Age When Born (Parent Age Trick)

**Signature:** "Father was X years old when son was born"

This means: **Father's age − Son's age = X (always)**

**Example:**
Father was 28 when son was born. Son is 15 now.
Father's age = 15 + 28 = **43** ✅ (one line!)

---

## 🔴 PATTERN 4 — The Average Age Replacement

**Signature:** "When A leaves/joins, average changes by d"

**Formula:**
```
Entering person's age = Old Average + d × (new group size)
Leaving person's age  = Old Average − d × (old group size)
```

Wait — simpler version:
- If avg increases by d after someone joins → new person's age = old avg + d×n (where n = new count)

**Example:**
Group of 6, avg = 20. New person joins, avg = 21.
New person's age = 21×7 − 20×6 = 147 − 120 = **27** ✅

---

## 🔴 PATTERN 5 — Three People Chain

**Signature:** A is X years older than B. B is Y years older than C.

**Shortcut:**
- B = C + Y
- A = B + X = C + Y + X

**Example:**
A is 5 older than B. B is 3 older than C. C = 10.
→ B = 13, A = 18 ✅ (read and write, 2 seconds)

---

## 🔴 PATTERN 6 — The "Year of Birth" Disguise

**Signature:** "Born in year X. Question asks about year Y."

**Formula:** Age in year Y = Y − X

**Example:**
Born in 1995. Age in 2025 = 2025 − 1995 = **30** ✅

**Combo:** "In 2010 he was twice his son's age. Son born in 2000. Find father's birth year."

Son's age in 2010 = 10 → Father's age in 2010 = 20
Father born = 2010 − 20 = **1990** ✅

---

## 🔴 PATTERN 7 — The Tricky "Ratio Was X, Now Y" Problem

**Signature:** "Some years ago ratio was A:B. Now it's C:D."
**This is the hardest type — but still has a pattern!**

Let present ages = Ck and Dk

n years ago: (Ck−n)/(Dk−n) = A/B

Cross multiply:
B(Ck−n) = A(Dk−n)
BCk − Bn = ADk − An
k(BC − AD) = Bn − An
k(BC − AD) = n(B − A)
**k = n(B−A) / (BC−AD)**

Once k found → present ages = Ck and Dk.
