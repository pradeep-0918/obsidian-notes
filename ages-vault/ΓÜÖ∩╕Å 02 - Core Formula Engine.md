## 🔧 The 3 Master Formulas

### 🔴 Formula 1 — The Time Shift Formula

```
Past Age   = Present Age − n        (n years ago)
Future Age = Present Age + n        (n years later)
```

**Example:**
Present age of Rahul = 25
- 5 years ago → 25 − 5 = **20**
- 8 years later → 25 + 8 = **33**

---

### 🔵 Formula 2 — The Equation Setup Formula

```
Let Present Age = x

Then:
  Past age (n years ago)    = x − n
  Future age (n years later) = x + n
```

**Template:**

> Always write: **"Let present age = x"** first.
> Then translate EVERY sentence of the problem into an equation.
> Solve for x.

---

### 🟢 Formula 3 — The Ratio Formula

```
If ages are in ratio a : b
Then ages = ak and bk  (where k is a common multiplier)
```

**Example:**
Ages of A and B are in ratio 3:5. Sum = 40.
→ 3k + 5k = 40
→ 8k = 40
→ k = 5
→ A = 15, B = 25 ✅

## 🏗️ The Universal Problem-Solving Framework

```
Step 1: IDENTIFY → Who? When? What to find?
Step 2: ASSIGN   → Let age = x (or ak, bk for ratios)
Step 3: TRANSLATE → Convert sentences to equations
Step 4: SOLVE    → Algebra
Step 5: VERIFY   → Plug back into original problem
```

---

## 📋 Worked Example — Full Method

> **Problem:** "The present age of father is 3 times his son's age. 12 years later, father will be twice the son's age. Find their present ages."

**Step 1: Identify**
- Who: Father, Son
- When: Present + Future (12 years later)
- Find: Present ages

**Step 2: Assign**
- Let son's present age = x
- Then father's present age = 3x

**Step 3: Translate**
- 12 years later: Son = x + 12, Father = 3x + 12
- Condition: Father = twice Son
- → 3x + 12 = 2(x + 12)

**Step 4: Solve**
```
3x + 12 = 2x + 24
3x − 2x = 24 − 12
x = 12
```

**Step 5: Verify**
- Son = 12, Father = 36 ✅
- 12 years later: Son = 24, Father = 48 = 2 × 24 ✅

**Answer: Son = 12 years, Father = 36 years**

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes — Avoid These!

| Mistake | Wrong | Right |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Adding instead of subtracting for past | x + 5 | x − 5 |
| Forgetting both ages shift | Only changing one person's age | Change BOTH people |
| Ratio not multiplied by k | Using ratio directly as age | Always use ak, bk |
