## 🗂️ The 5 Question Types

Every exam blood relation question falls into **exactly one** of these 5 types.
Master each type → crack any question.

---

## TYPE 1 — 🔗 Chain Relation (Most Common!)

**What it looks like:**
> "A is the son of B. B is the brother of C. C is the mother of D. How is D related to A?"

**How to Solve:**
1. Draw a vertical/horizontal tree as you read each clue
2. Place each person in their correct position
3. Trace from start to end

**Solved Example:**
```
Q: A is the father of B. B is the sister of C. C is the wife of D.
   D is the son of E. How is E related to A?

TREE:
    A (father)
    │
    B (daughter) ─── C (sister)
                     │ (wife of)
                     D (husband)
                     │ (son of)
                     E

Path: A → B → C → D → E
- A is B's father
- C is B's sister → C is also A's daughter
- D is C's husband → D is A's son-in-law
- E is D's father → E is A's son-in-law's father

✅ Answer: E is A's son-in-law's father
   (Some exams say: "Cannot be determined" if gender is unclear — be careful!)
```

## TYPE 2 — 📸 Pointing to a Photograph

**What it looks like:**
> "Pointing to a photograph, Meena says: 'He is the son of the only daughter of my grandfather.' Who is the person in the photo to Meena?"

**How to Solve:**
1. Start from the **speaker** (Meena)
2. Build the tree **backwards** from the photo person
3. Connect to the speaker

**Solved Example:**
```
Q: Pointing to a man, Priya says, "His mother is the only daughter of my mother."
   How is the man related to Priya?

CLUE BREAKDOWN:
- "My mother's only daughter" = PRIYA herself
- So man's mother = PRIYA
- Therefore: Man = Priya's SON

✅ Answer: Son
```

**⚡ SPEED TRICK for Photo questions:**
> If "X is the only son/daughter of Y" → check if Y = the speaker's direct parent
> If yes, the photo person is the speaker's **sibling's child or own child**

---

## TYPE 3 — 🧩 Puzzle / Statement Based

**What it looks like:**
> "A, B, C, D, E are five members of a family. Two married couples. B is the son of C and brother of D. E is the wife of C. D is the unmarried sister. Who are the married couples?"

**How to Solve:**
1. List all members
2. Mark gender from clues (M/F)
3. Mark married pairs
4. Draw complete tree at end

**Solved Example:**
```
Members: A, B, C, D, E

Clues:
- B is son of C → B(M), C is parent
- B is brother of D → D is sibling (D = F since D is "sister")
- E is wife of C → C(M), E(F) → First couple: C + E
- D is unmarried sister
- A = only remaining person → A must be another parent figure

TREE:
    C(M) ══ E(F)      A(?)
         │
    B(M)   D(F)

If A is grandmother → A(F)
Married Couples: C & E, and A & (C's father — not given)

✅ Married Couples: C + E (clearly stated)
```

## TYPE 4 — 🔐 Coded Blood Relations

**What it looks like:**
> "A + B means A is the father of B"
> "A – B means A is the wife of B"
> "A × B means A is the brother of B"
> "If P + Q – R × S, how is P related to S?"

**Full Guide → [[05_Coded_Blood_Relations]]**

**Quick Approach:**
1. Replace symbols with words
2. Build the tree
3. Trace P to S

---

## TYPE 5 — ❓ Find the Odd One / Gender Based

**What it looks like:**
> "If X is the brother of Y, Y is the sister of Z, Z is the son of W — how many males are there?"

**How to Solve:**
1. List all gender clues
2. Mark each person M or F
3. Count / identify as asked

**Solved Example:**
```
Q: A is the sister of B. B is the brother of C. C is the son of D. D is the wife of E.
   How many males are there?

- A = F (sister)
- B = M (brother)
- C = M (son)
- D = F (wife)
- E = M (husband)

✅ Males: B, C, E → 3 males
```

## 🆚 Quick Comparison Table

| Type | Key Word to Spot | Speed Strategy |
|------|-----------------|----------------|
| Chain Relation | "is the son/father/sister of" | Draw as you read |
| Photo Pointing | "Pointing to a photo/man/woman" | Start from speaker |
| Puzzle/Statement | "Five members", "two couples" | List + mark gender |
| Coded | "+, –, ×, ÷, @, $" symbols | Decode first, draw second |
| Count/Gender | "How many males/females" | Mark M/F for each name |

---

## ⚡ 10-Second Identification Rule

When you see a question, scan for:
1. **Symbols?** → Type 4 (Coded)
2. **"Pointing to"?** → Type 2 (Photo)
3. **Multiple members listed?** → Type 3 (Puzzle)
4. **"How many males/females?"** → Type 5 (Gender Count)
5. **Everything else?** → Type 1 (Chain)
