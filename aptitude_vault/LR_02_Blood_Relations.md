## 🧠 Concept

Blood relation questions test your ability to decode family relationships from clues.

**Key insight:** Always draw a **family tree**. Never try to solve in your head!

## 🛠️ The Tree Method (ALWAYS USE THIS)

### Drawing Rules
- **Male** → Square ☐ or (M)
- **Female** → Circle ○ or (F)
- **Marriage/Couple** → Horizontal line between them
- **Parent → Child** → Vertical line down
- **Siblings** → Connected by horizontal line from same parent

### Example
> "A is B's father, B is C's sister, C is D's daughter."
```
A (M) ─── ? (F)
      |
   ───┼───
   |     |
   B(?)  C(F)─── D(?)
```
Wait — C is D's daughter, so D is C's parent. And A is B's father.
B is C's sister → B and C have same parents (A is their father).
D is C's mother (or father).
Since A is the father and D is C's parent but not mentioned as A...
→ D could be the mother = A's wife.
→ D is A's wife. D is related to A as **Wife**.

## 🔥 Types of Questions

### Type 1: Find direct relationship
> "A is B's father's only son. How is B related to A?"
- A is the only son of B's father → A IS B's father's son → A IS B's brother
- OR: A is the only son → B has only one uncle/brother via father
- **B is A's nephew/niece** (A is B's uncle) ← This depends on further context
- Actually: "A is B's father's only son" → A = B's father's son = B's brother
- So B is A's **sibling** (brother or sister)

### Type 2: Coded Relations
> "If A + B means A is father of B, A − B means A is mother of B, A × B means A is brother of B, A ÷ B means A is sister of B. P + Q − R means?"
- P + Q: P is father of Q
- Q − R: Q is mother of R
- So: P is father of Q, Q is mother of R → P is grandfather of R
- R is P's **grandchild**

### Type 3: Pointing/Introducing
> "Pointing to a man, a woman says 'His mother is the only daughter of my mother.' How is the woman related to the man?"
- Only daughter of my mother = the woman herself
- His mother = the woman
- So the woman IS his mother → **Mother**

### Type 4: Multi-step chain
> "A is B's father, B is C's sister, D is C's mother, E is D's father. How is E related to A?"
- Build tree: A is father of B and C (since B is C's sister)
- D is C's mother → D is A's wife
- E is D's father → E is A's father-in-law
- E is A's **father-in-law**

## 💡 Shortcuts & Tricks

### Trick 1: "Only son/daughter"
> "X is the only son of Y's father" → X = Y's father's only son
> This means X IS Y's father himself (if Y has no brothers) OR X is Y's brother
> Context decides — look for additional clues

### Trick 2: Gender Determination
- If no gender mentioned and relationship is ambiguous, look for pronouns (he/she/his/her)
- "Son" → Male, "Daughter" → Female
- "Parent" → Either gender

### Trick 3: Pointing Questions
> "That man's mother's husband" → Man's father
> "My son's father" = I (the speaker) if speaker is the father
> "My mother's only son" = Me (the speaker)

### Trick 4: In-law Relations
- Husband's side → add "in-law" to corresponding direct relation
- Wife's brother = Brother-in-law; Wife's mother = Mother-in-law

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

1. **Confusing "nephew" and "cousin"**
   - Nephew = brother's or sister's son
   - Cousin = uncle's or aunt's child

2. **Gender errors in chain reasoning**
   - Track genders carefully, especially when not stated

3. **"Only child" implications**
   - "Only son" = no other male children (but may have sisters)
   - "Only child" = no siblings at all

4. **Maternal vs Paternal**
   - Mother's brother ≠ Father's brother (both are uncles, but different sides)

5. **Not drawing the tree**
   - In long chains (4+ people), ALWAYS draw before answering

## 🧩 Practice Questions

### Medium
1. A is B's father, B is C's sister, C is D's daughter. How is D related to A?
   > B and C are siblings; A is B's (and C's) father; D is C's mother → D is A's wife → **Wife**

2. P is Q's mother, Q is R's sister, R is S's daughter. How is S related to P?
   > R and Q are siblings; P is Q's (and R's) mother; S is R's parent → S could be the father
   > S is P's **husband** (assuming S is the father)

3. A is B's brother, C is D's sister, D is A's wife. How is C related to B?
   > A and B are brothers; D is A's wife; C is D's sister → C is A's sister-in-law
   > Since A and B are brothers, C is also B's **sister-in-law**

4. A is B's wife, C is B's son, D is C's sister. How is D related to A?
   > B and A are married; C and D are B's children; D is A's **daughter**

5. P is Q's father, Q is R's brother, R is S's son. How is S related to P?
   > Q and R are brothers; P is their father; S is R's (and Q's) parent
   > S is the mother → S is P's **wife**

6. A is B's mother, C is B's daughter, D is C's father. How is D related to A?
   > A is B's mother; C is B's daughter → B is parent to C; D is C's father = B's husband (or B's partner)
   > D is B's husband; A is B's mother → D is A's **son-in-law**

7. P is Q's brother, Q is R's son, R is S's husband. How is S related to P?
   > Q's parent = R; R's spouse = S; So P is Q's brother = R and S's son
   > S is P's **mother**

### Difficult
1. A is B's father, C is B's sister, D is A's wife, E is C's son. How is E related to D?
   > A and D are married; B and C are their children; E is C's son → E is A and D's grandchild
   > E is D's **grandson**

2. P is Q's brother, Q is R's son, R is S's husband, T is S's daughter. How is T related to P?
   > R and S are married; Q and T are their children (Q is son, T is daughter); P is Q's brother
   > So P is also R and S's son; T is R and S's daughter → T is P's **sister**

3. A is B's mother, C is B's son, D is C's sister, E is A's husband. How is E related to C?
   > A and E are married; B is their child; C and D are B's children
   > E is B's father; C is B's son → E is C's **grandfather**

4. P is Q's father, Q is R's sister, R is S's daughter, T is S's husband. How is T related to Q?
   > Q and R are siblings (P is their father); S is R's (and Q's) parent = the mother
   > T is S's husband = the father? But P is Q's father... 
   > So P = T (same person) OR S is Q's mother and T = P = father
   > T is Q's **father** (T = P)

5. A is B's wife, C is B's son, D is C's sister, E is A's daughter. How is E related to C?
   > A and B are married; C and D are their children; E is A's daughter = also B's child
   > E = D (both daughters of A and B) OR E is another child
   > E is C's **sister**
