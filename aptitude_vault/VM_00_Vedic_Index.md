## 🌟 What is Vedic Mathematics?

Vedic Mathematics is a collection of **16 Sutras (formulas)** and **13 Sub-Sutras** rediscovered from ancient Indian Vedic texts by **Sri Bharati Krishna Tirthaji** (1884–1960) and published in his book *"Vedic Mathematics"* (1965).

These sutras encode **mental calculation shortcuts** that allow you to solve problems that normally take 30–60 seconds in under **5–10 seconds** — entirely in your head.

> 💡 **Why learn Vedic Maths for competitive exams?**
> - Multiply 2-digit numbers without writing a single step
> - Square any number instantly
> - Check answers in 2 seconds using digit sums
> - Solve in your head while others are still picking up their pen

## 📜 The 16 Vedic Sutras — Master Table

| # | Sutra (Sanskrit) | English Meaning | Main Applications |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ekadhikena Purvena** | By one more than the previous | Squaring numbers ending in 5, recurring decimals |
| 2 | **Nikhilam Navatashcaramam Dashatah** | All from 9, last from 10 | Multiplication near base (10, 100, 1000) |
| 3 | **Urdhva-Tiryagbyham** | Vertically and crosswise | Universal multiplication (any digits) |
| 4 | **Paravartya Yojayet** | Transpose and apply | Division, transposition |
| 5 | **Shunyam Saamyasamuccaye** | If sum is same, it is zero | Linear equations |
| 6 | **Anurupyena** | Proportionality | Multiplication near multiple of base |
| 7 | **Sankalana-Vyavakalanabhyam** | By addition and subtraction | Systems of equations |
| 8 | **Puranapuranabhyam** | By completion and non-completion | Quadratic equations |
| 9 | **Chalana-Kalanabyham** | Differences and similarities | Quadratics, factorization |
| 10 | **Yavadunam** | Whatever the extent of deficiency | Squares of numbers near base |
| 11 | **Vyashtisamanstih** | Specific and general | Factorization |
| 12 | **Shesanyankena Caramena** | Remainders by the last digit | Conversion, fractions |
| 13 | **Sopaantyadvayamantyam** | Ultimate and twice penultimate | Partial fractions |
| 14 | **Ekanyunena Purvena** | By one less than the previous | Multiplication by 9, 99, 999... |
| 15 | **Gunitasamuchyah** | Product of sum = sum of products | Verification |
| 16 | **Gunakasamuchyah** | — | Equation verification |

## ⚡ Sutra Cheat Sheet for Exams

### When to use which Sutra:

```
Multiplying near 100 (e.g., 97×96)?
→ NIKHILAM (All from 9, last from 10)

Multiplying any 2×2 digits (e.g., 43×67)?
→ URDHVA-TIRYAK (Vertically & Crosswise)

Squaring number ending in 5 (e.g., 75²)?
→ EKADHIKENA PURVENA (By one more)

Multiplying by 9, 99, 999?
→ EKANYUNENA (By one less)

Multiplying near multiple of 100 (e.g., 212×213)?
→ ANURUPYENA (Proportionality)

Verifying your answer?
→ DIGIT SUM METHOD (Gunitasamuchyah)
```
