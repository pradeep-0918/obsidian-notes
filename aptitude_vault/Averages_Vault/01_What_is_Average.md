## 🧒 Feynman Explanation (Like You're 6)

Imagine **5 friends** share **15 pizza slices**.  
How many slices does **each person** get fairly?

```
15 slices ÷ 5 friends = 3 slices each
```

**That's the average. It's the FAIR SHARE number.**

> 🧠 Average = "If everything was distributed equally, what would each person get?"

---

## 🔍 Real Life Examples

| Situation | Average Means |
|-----------|--------------|
| 5 students scored different marks | What would each score if marks were equally shared? |
| You drive at different speeds each hour | What single speed represents the whole trip? |
| Different ages of people in a room | What's the "middle" age? |

---

## 📦 The 3-Word Definition

> **Average = Levelling Everything Out**

Think of it like water in unequal buckets — pour them all into one big bucket, then distribute equally. That equal level = **average**.

---

## 🎯 Why Do We Use Average?

1. **Compare** groups fairly (Which class did better?)
2. **Predict** (If I average 80 now, can I get 90 by end of year?)
3. **Summarize** big data into ONE number
