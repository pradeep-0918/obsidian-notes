## 🟡 Level 2 — Intermediate (Q11–Q25)

**Q11.** Class A (20 students) avg = 70. Class B (30 students) avg = 80. Combined avg?
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
Sum A = 20×70 = 1400  
Sum B = 30×80 = 2400  
Combined = (1400+2400)/50 = 3800/50 = **76** ✅  
*Weighted Average: [[04_Weighted_Average]]*
</details>

---

**Q12.** Average of 10 numbers = 20. One number is wrongly written as 13 instead of 31. Correct average?
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
Error = 31−13 = 18  
Correct avg = 20 + 18/10 = 20 + 1.8 = **21.8** ✅  
*See [[06_Missing_Number_Problems]] Example 3*
</details>

---

**Q13.** Average of 5 numbers = 40. A 6th number is added, average becomes 42. Find 6th number.
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
New member = 42 + 5×(42−40) = 42 + 10 = **52** ✅  
*Trick 6 from [[03_Shortcut_Tricks]]*
</details>

**Q15.** Average age of 40 students = 15. Teacher included, average = 15.5. Teacher's age?

**Q16.** Average of 6 numbers = 30. 5 of them are 25, 28, 32, 35, 27. Find 6th.
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
Total = 6×30 = 180  
Sum of 5 = 25+28+32+35+27 = 147  
6th = 180−147 = **33** ✅
</details>

**Q17.** Average weight of 8 persons = 60 kg. One person (50 kg) is replaced. New avg = 62.5. Find new person's weight.
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
New = 50 + 8×(62.5−60) = 50 + 8×2.5 = 50+20 = **70 kg** ✅  
*Replacement formula: [[08_Replacement_Problems]]*
</details>

**Q18.** Average of 4 consecutive even numbers is 27. Find the largest.
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
4 consecutive even: n-3, n-1, n+1, n+3  
Average = n (middle of the group) = 27  
Actually avg = (n-3+n+3)/2 = 27  
Numbers: 24, 26, 28, 30 → Largest = **30** ✅  
Check: (24+26+28+30)/4 = 108/4 = 27 ✅
</details>

**Q19.** The average of 12 results is 40 and average of first 4 is 32, last 4 is 44. Average of middle 4?
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
Total sum = 12×40 = 480  
First 4 sum = 4×32 = 128  
Last 4 sum = 4×44 = 176  
Middle 4 sum = 480−128−176 = 176  
Middle avg = 176/4 = **44** ✅
</details>

**Q22.** A student scores average 60 in 9 exams. In 10th exam, how much should he score to bring average to 65?
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
Required 10th score = 65 + 9×(65−60) = 65+45 = **110**  
*(Hmm, 110 > 100, which shows it's mathematically impossible if max is 100.)*  
Standard method: New sum = 10×65 = 650  
Old sum = 9×60 = 540  
10th score = 650−540 = **110** ✅
</details>

**Q25.** Average of a, b = 25. Average of b, c = 35. Average of a, c = 30. Find a, b, c.
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
a+b = 50 ... (i)  
b+c = 70 ... (ii)  
a+c = 60 ... (iii)  
Add all: 2(a+b+c) = 180 → a+b+c = 90  
a = 90−70 = 20  
b = 90−60 = 30  
c = 90−50 = 40  
**a=20, b=30, c=40** ✅
</details>

## 🔴 Level 3 — Advanced (Q26–Q35)

**Q26.** Average speed for A→B at 30 km/h and return at 60 km/h?
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
Equal distance → Harmonic Mean  
Avg = 2×30×60/(30+60) = 3600/90 = **40 km/h** ✅  
*See [[09_Speed_Distance_Average]]*
</details>

---

**Q27.** A batsman's average for 40 innings = 50. His highest score is 172 more than lowest. Excluding both, avg = 48. Find highest and lowest.
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
Total = 40×50 = 2000  
Total (38 innings) = 38×48 = 1824  
H + L = 2000 − 1824 = 176  
H − L = 172  
Solving: H = 174, L = 2  
**Highest = 174, Lowest = 2** ✅
</details>

---

**Q28.** Average of 50 numbers = 38. Two numbers 45 and 55 are removed. Find new average.
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
Old sum = 50×38 = 1900  
Remove 45+55 = 100  
New sum = 1800  
New avg = 1800/48 = **37.5** ✅
</details>

**Q29.** In a group of 16, if 1 person of 60 kg is replaced by 4 people, average drops by 3 kg. Find avg weight of 4 new people.
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
Old sum = 16×X  
New group = 19 people  
Drop in average = 3  
Old sum = 16X, New sum = 19(X−3) = 19X−57  
Difference = 3 new people added, person removed = 60  
Sum added by 4 − 60 = 19X−57 − 16X = 3X−57  
Hmm — let's use simpler approach:  
Let old avg = A  
Old sum = 16A  
New sum = 19(A−3)  
Sum added by 4 = New sum − Old sum + 60  
= 19A−57 − 16A + 60 = 3A+3  
Avg of 4 new = (3A+3)/4  
*This needs A — so assuming A = 57 (given avg drops by 3 to 54)*  
Avg of 4 = (3×57+3)/4 = 174/4 = **43.5 kg** ✅
</details>

---

**Q30.** A man travels 30 km at 6 km/h, 60 km at 12 km/h, and 90 km at 18 km/h. Average speed?
<details>
<summary>💡 Solution</summary>
Time 1 = 30/6 = 5 hrs  
Time 2 = 60/12 = 5 hrs  
Time 3 = 90/18 = 5 hrs  
Total dist = 180, Total time = 15  
Avg = 180/15 = **12 km/h** ✅
</details>
