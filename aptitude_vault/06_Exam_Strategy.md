## 🎯 The Core Mindset

> "The exam doesn't test how much you know. It tests how efficiently you use what you know under time pressure."

Three pillars of exam success:
1. **Accuracy** — Right answers, not just attempts
2. **Speed** — Maximum questions in minimum time
3. **Strategy** — Which questions to attempt and which to skip

---

## ⏱️ Time Management Framework

### The 90-Second Rule
- Budget: 90 seconds per question for aptitude
- If you haven't cracked it in 90 sec → **MARK, SKIP, MOVE ON**
- Return at the end if time permits

### The 3-Tier Approach

**Tier 1 (0–30 sec):** Quick wins — solve immediately
- Series, analogies, basic coding-decoding
- Simple averages, percentages, profit/loss

**Tier 2 (30–90 sec):** Medium effort
- Time & work, SI/CI, direction sense
- Syllogism, blood relations

**Tier 3 (90–240 sec):** Complex problems — attempt last
- Seating arrangements, puzzles
- Hard P&C, probability
- Complex HCF/LCM

**Strategy:** 
1. First pass → solve all Tier 1 (fast easy marks)
2. Second pass → solve Tier 2 questions
3. Third pass → attempt best Tier 3 questions

## 🧮 Mental Math Speed Techniques

### Technique 1: Percentage Quick Calculation
- 10%: Move decimal one left (₹3,500 → 10% = ₹350)
- 5%: Half of 10% (₹350 → ₹175)
- 15%: 10% + 5% (₹350 + ₹175 = ₹525)
- 25%: Divide by 4
- 33%: Divide by 3
- 50%: Divide by 2

### Technique 2: Multiplication Shortcuts
- ×11: Add adjacent digits (35 × 11 = 3(3+5)5 = 385)
- ×25: ÷4 × 100 (48 × 25 = 48/4 × 100 = 1200)
- ×99: ×100 − number (63 × 99 = 6300 − 63 = 6237)
- ×125: ÷8 × 1000 (24 × 125 = 24/8 × 1000 = 3000)

### Technique 3: Squaring Near Multiples of 10
- 95² = (100−5)² = 10000 − 1000 + 25 = 9025
- 48² = (50−2)² = 2500 − 200 + 4 = 2304
- 103² = (100+3)² = 10000 + 600 + 9 = 10609

### Technique 4: Division Shortcuts
- Divide by 5: Multiply by 2, divide by 10 (73 ÷ 5 = 146 ÷ 10 = 14.6)
- Divide by 25: Multiply by 4, divide by 100
- Divide by 125: Multiply by 8, divide by 1000

## 🔍 Question Reading Strategy

### Before Solving
1. **Read the question first** (not options) — understand what's ASKED
2. **Identify the type** — Average? Speed? Work?
3. **Note units** — km/h or m/s? Years or months?
4. **Check for traps** — "how many more", "what percentage decrease"

### After Getting an Answer
1. **Sanity check:** Does the answer make sense?
   > "Average speed = 250 km/h" → Way too high for a person walking!
2. **Re-read the question:** Did they ask for profit or profit%?
3. **Check options:** If your answer isn't close to any option → recalculate

---

## 🎯 Option Elimination Techniques

### Technique 1: Boundary Elimination
> "Average speed between 60 and 80 km/h when equal distances" → Must be between 60 and 80, NOT 70
> Eliminate options outside the logical range

### Technique 2: Parity Check
> "Sum of 5 odd numbers" → Always odd. Eliminate even options.
> "Profit% must be positive" → Eliminate negative answers if profit context is clear.

### Technique 3: The "Obviously Wrong" Filter
For percentage problems: If original = ₹100 and increased by 20%, answer can't be ₹80.

### Technique 4: Plug-in Verification
For algebra: Substitute answer back into the problem to verify.
> "If speed = 50 km/h answers the question, test: 300/50 − 300/60 = 6−5 = 1 ✓"

## 🏆 Topic-Specific Tactics

### Average
- **Before calculating:** Estimate — is the answer closer to the lower or higher value?
- **Trick:** Assume the average and work with deviations

### Time & Distance
- **Draw a timeline** for problems with two people
- **Unit conversion first** — always convert before calculating
- **For average speed:** Never add speeds unless equal time is spent

### Time & Work
- **Use LCM method always** — eliminates fractions
- **State work as units** not fractions

### Profit & Loss
- **Assume CP = 100** when CP is not given
- **Marked price problems:** Always trace CP → MP → SP

### SI & CI
- **Multiplier table on hand:** 1.1²=1.21, 1.1³=1.331, 1.2²=1.44
- **For "doubles in n years CI":** Use rule of 72 to estimate

### Syllogism
- **Draw Venn diagrams** — don't solve in head
- **Check ALL possible diagrams** — conclusion must hold for ALL valid diagrams

### Seating Arrangement
- **Fix one person first** (especially in circular)
- **Fill definite clues first, negative clues last**

## ⚠️ Trap Avoidance Guide

| Trap Type | Example | Correct Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Wrong base for % | Profit% on SP not CP | Always % on CP |
| Same % gain/loss, same SP | "No net change" | There's always a loss |
| Average of averages | Simple mean of two means | Weighted mean required |
| Time in wrong unit | Speed=60km/h, time=30 min | Convert to hours first |
| Circular vs Linear left/right | Different in each | Specify before solving |
| "How much more" vs "how much" | "5 is how much more than 3? = 2" vs "what is the value = 5" | Read carefully |
| HCF vs LCM confusion | "Greatest" → HCF; "smallest divisible" → LCM | Match words to operation |

## 🏁 During the Exam

### The 3-Pass System
```
Pass 1 (First 50%): 
→ Solve only questions you can finish in < 60 seconds
→ Mark others to return

Pass 2 (Next 35%):
→ Tackle medium-difficulty questions
→ Skip if exceeding 90 seconds

Pass 3 (Last 15%):
→ Hard questions + review marked questions
→ Make educated guesses if negative marking is absent
```

### Marking Guesses
If NO negative marking: Attempt ALL questions — educated guess beats blank.
If negative marking: Only guess when you can eliminate 2+ options.

## 📊 Post-Exam Analysis (For Future Tests)

After every mock/test:
1. **Categorize errors:**
   - Careless mistake (knew how, made error)
   - Concept gap (didn't know the method)
   - Time pressure (knew but ran out of time)
2. **Fix by type:**
   - Careless → More careful reading habits
   - Concept gap → Re-study the topic
   - Time → Speed drills for that topic
3. **Track pattern:** Which topics cause most errors? → Increase practice there.
