## 🧒 The Simple Idea

Imagine you're in a car at 60 km/h.
Another car passes you at 80 km/h.

From **your** point of view, how fast is the other car moving?

> You feel it moving at: 80 - 60 = **20 km/h** (forward, relative to you)

That's relative speed! It's all about **perspective**.

---

## 🚀 The Two Golden Rules

### Rule 1: Moving in OPPOSITE Directions
$$\text{Relative Speed} = S_1 + S_2$$

> Think: Two trains rushing toward each other. They close the gap FAST!
> Combined speed = add them up.

### Rule 2: Moving in SAME Direction
$$\text{Relative Speed} = |S_1 - S_2|$$

> Think: A faster car overtaking a slower car. The gap closes slowly.
> Effective speed = subtract them.

---

## 🎯 The Dog & Man Memory Trick

🐕 Dog runs **toward** man → They meet quickly → **ADD**
🐕 Dog runs **same way** as man → Dog slowly catches up → **SUBTRACT**

---

## 📊 Relative Speed Summary Table

| Scenario | Relative Speed | Time to Meet/Cross |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Opposite directions | S₁ + S₂ | d / (S₁+S₂) |
| Same direction | \|S₁ - S₂\| | d / \|S₁-S₂\| |
| One stationary | Other's speed | d / S |

## 🧪 Solved Examples

### Example 1 — Meeting Point (Opposite Directions)
> A and B start from two towns 300 km apart and walk toward each other at 10 km/h and 15 km/h. When do they meet?

Relative Speed = 10 + 15 = 25 km/h
Time = 300 / 25 = **12 hours** ✅

---

### Example 2 — Overtaking (Same Direction)
> A thief at 10 km/h runs. Police at 15 km/h chases from 50 km behind. When does police catch thief?

Relative Speed = 15 - 10 = 5 km/h
Time = 50 / 5 = **10 hours** ✅

---

### Example 3 — Where Do They Meet?
> A runs at 6 km/h, B runs at 4 km/h from opposite ends of 100 km road. Where do they meet?

Meet time = 100 / (6+4) = 10 hours
Distance covered by A = 6 × 10 = **60 km from A's side** ✅

> ⚡ Shortcut: They split the distance in ratio of speeds!
> A : B = 6 : 4 = 3 : 2
> A covers 3/5 × 100 = **60 km** ← Same answer, 5× faster!

---

### Example 4 — Two Trains Crossing (Opposite)
> Train A (200m) at 72 km/h and Train B (300m) at 108 km/h approach each other. Time to cross?

Convert speeds: 72 km/h = 20 m/s, 108 km/h = 30 m/s
Relative Speed = 20 + 30 = 50 m/s
Total distance to cross = 200 + 300 = 500 m
Time = 500 / 50 = **10 seconds** ✅

---

### Example 5 — Circular Track (Meeting Again)
> A and B run around a 400m track. A at 5 m/s, B at 3 m/s. Same start, same direction. When do they meet again?

Relative Speed = 5 - 3 = 2 m/s
A laps B when A has covered 400m MORE than B.
Time = 400 / 2 = **200 seconds** ✅

---

### Example 6 — Circular Track (Opposite)
> Same track, opposite directions.

Relative Speed = 5 + 3 = 8 m/s
They meet when combined distance = 400m
Time = 400 / 8 = **50 seconds** ✅

---

## ⚡ Speed Tricks

### Trick 1: Ratio Split Trick
When two people start from opposite ends:
> Distance covered = in ratio of their speeds

A : B = S_A : S_B

### Trick 2: Time to Meet vs Time to Lap
- Meeting (opposite) = Track length / Sum of speeds
- Lapping (same) = Track length / Difference of speeds

### Trick 3: The "Police-Thief" Shortcut
> Head start = H km, speed difference = ΔS
> Time to catch = H / ΔS

---

## 🚨 Watch Out For!

- When they start at **different times** → find position of one when the other starts, THEN apply relative speed
- When asked "how far from start?" → find meeting time, then multiply by one person's actual speed

## ✅ Self-Test

1. Two trains (same direction): 60 km/h and 90 km/h, gap = 180 km. Time to close gap?
2. Two cyclists 120 km apart, riding toward each other: 20 km/h and 40 km/h. Time to meet?
3. A and B on 600m circular track, speeds 8 m/s and 4 m/s opposite ways. First meeting after?
4. Thief starts 100m ahead. Thief: 8 m/s, Police: 10 m/s. Time to catch?

> **Answers:**
> 1) 180/(90-60) = 6 hours
> 2) 120/60 = 2 hours
> 3) 600/12 = 50 seconds
> 4) 100/2 = 50 seconds
