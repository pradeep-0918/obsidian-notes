## 🧒 The Feynman Explanation

Imagine you're swimming in a river.

- When you swim **WITH** the current (downstream), the water pushes you → you go **FASTER**
- When you swim **AGAINST** the current (upstream), the water fights you → you go **SLOWER**

The river's current speed = **Stream speed**
Your speed in still water = **Boat speed**

---

## 🔑 The 4 Core Formulas

Let:
- **B** = Boat speed in still water
- **S** = Stream speed (current speed)
- **D** = Downstream speed (with current)
- **U** = Upstream speed (against current)

### Formula 1: Downstream Speed
$$D = B + S$$

> Current helps you → add stream speed

### Formula 2: Upstream Speed
$$U = B - S$$

> Current fights you → subtract stream speed

### Formula 3: Boat Speed (Still Water)
$$B = \frac{D + U}{2}$$

### Formula 4: Stream Speed
$$S = \frac{D - U}{2}$$

---

## 🧠 Memory Device — "DUBS"

**D**own = **B** + **S** (going DOWN the river, speeds add UP)
**U**p = **B** - **S** (going UP against river, speed goes DOWN)
**B** = (D+U)/2 (average of both)
**S** = (D-U)/2 (half the difference)

## 🧪 Solved Examples

### Example 1 — Basic
> Boat speed = 10 km/h, Stream = 3 km/h. Find downstream and upstream speeds.

Downstream = 10 + 3 = **13 km/h**
Upstream = 10 - 3 = **7 km/h** ✅

---

### Example 2 — Find Boat & Stream Speed
> A boat goes 36 km downstream in 2 hours and returns upstream in 3 hours.

Downstream speed = 36/2 = 18 km/h
Upstream speed = 36/3 = 12 km/h

Boat speed = (18+12)/2 = **15 km/h**
Stream speed = (18-12)/2 = **3 km/h** ✅

---

### Example 3 — Time Ratio Trick
> A boat covers same distance upstream and downstream. Time ratio upstream:downstream = 3:2.
> Find ratio of boat speed to stream speed.

Let U and D be speeds.
Same distance → time inversely proportional to speed.
So D/U = Time_U/Time_D = 3/2

Let D = 3k, U = 2k
B = (3k+2k)/2 = 5k/2
S = (3k-2k)/2 = k/2

B/S = (5k/2)/(k/2) = **5:1** ✅

> 🎯 **General Formula:** If Time ratio U:D = m:n → B:S = (m+n):(m-n)

---

### Example 4 — Distance Problem
> A boat goes upstream 15 km in 3 hrs and downstream 20 km in 2 hrs.
> Find: speed in still water and stream speed.

Upstream speed = 15/3 = 5 km/h
Downstream speed = 20/2 = 10 km/h

B = (10+5)/2 = **7.5 km/h**
S = (10-5)/2 = **2.5 km/h** ✅

---

### Example 5 — Round Trip Time
> Boat speed = 8 km/h, stream = 2 km/h. Time to go 30 km downstream and come back?

Downstream = 30/10 = 3 hours
Upstream = 30/6 = 5 hours
Total = **8 hours** ✅

## ⚡ Killer Shortcuts

### Shortcut 1: Round Trip Speed
$$\text{Avg Speed} = \frac{2 \times D \times U}{D + U} = \frac{2(B+S)(B-S)}{2B} = \frac{B^2-S^2}{B}$$

### Shortcut 2: Time Ratio Formula
> Time_upstream / Time_downstream = D_speed / U_speed (same distance)

### Shortcut 3: If Stream Speed = 0
> Boat speed = both upstream and downstream (still water)
> This is the control case — use it to verify answers!

### Shortcut 4: Man in Water vs Boat
> Man falls overboard at point X.
> Boat turns around (upstream) and picks him up.
> In the time boat traveled upstream and downstream back to X, water moved man DOWNSTREAM.
> **Time up + Time down = same as if boat was still, man floated**
> Stream speed = Distance man floated / Total time

---

## 🧠 Advanced: Man Overboard Problem

> A boat goes upstream. A box falls overboard. Boat continues 1 hour, turns, meets box 6 km downstream from drop point. Find stream speed.

Let stream speed = s km/h, boat speed = b km/h
In 1 hour going up: boat position = start + (b-s)×1 upstream
Box floats downstream for 2 total hours (1 up + 1 down to meet): 6 = s × 2
**Stream speed = 3 km/h** ✅

(The boat traveling = irrelevant! Only the box travel matters = stream × total time)

## ✅ Self-Test

1. Downstream 40 km in 4 hrs, upstream 30 km in 6 hrs. Find B and S.
2. B = 12 km/h, S = 4 km/h. Time for 64 km downstream and back?
3. Time upstream: downstream = 5:3. Find B:S.
4. In still water B = 20 km/h, S = 5 km/h. Distance if upstream takes 3 hrs?

> **Answers:**
> 1) D=10, U=5; B=7.5, S=2.5
> 2) 64/16 + 64/8 = 4+8 = 12 hrs
> 3) (5+3):(5-3) = 8:2 = 4:1
> 4) (20-5)×3 = 45 km
