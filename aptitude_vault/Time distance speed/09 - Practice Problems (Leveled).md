## 🟢 LEVEL 1 — Basic (Warm Up)

**Q1.** A car travels at 60 km/h for 2.5 hours. Distance covered?

**Q2.** A train covers 360 km in 4 hours. Speed in m/s?

**Q3.** Convert 54 km/h to m/s.

**Q4.** A cyclist takes 30 minutes to cover 10 km. Speed in km/h?

**Q5.** A person walks 4 km in 1 hour. How long to walk 10 km?

---

### 🟢 Level 1 Answers

| Q | Answer | Solution |
|---|--------|----------|
| Q1 | 150 km | 60 × 2.5 = 150 |
| Q2 | 25 m/s | 360/4 = 90 km/h → 90×5/18 = 25 m/s |
| Q3 | 15 m/s | 54 × 5/18 = 15 |
| Q4 | 20 km/h | 10/(30/60) = 10/0.5 = 20 |
| Q5 | 2.5 hours | 10/4 = 2.5 |

## 🟡 LEVEL 2 — Medium (Core Concepts)

**Q6.** A car covers 100 km at 50 km/h and 200 km at 100 km/h. Average speed?

**Q7.** A and B start from opposite ends of a 150 km road. A at 30 km/h, B at 45 km/h. When and where do they meet?

**Q8.** A 300m train at 90 km/h crosses a 200m platform. Time?

**Q9.** A person arrives 20 min late at 3 km/h and 10 min early at 4 km/h. Find distance and correct time.

**Q10.** Downstream speed = 15 km/h, Upstream = 9 km/h. Find boat speed and stream speed.

**Q11.** A thief runs at 8 km/h. Police starts 15 min later at 12 km/h. When does police catch thief?

**Q12.** A and B run a 600m circular track at 5 m/s and 3 m/s in opposite directions. How many times do they meet in 10 minutes?

---

### 🟡 Level 2 Answers

**Q6:** Total D = 300 km. T₁ = 100/50 = 2h, T₂ = 200/100 = 2h. Total T = 4h. Avg = 300/4 = **75 km/h**

**Q7:** Relative speed = 75 km/h. Time = 150/75 = **2 hours**.
Meeting point: A covers 30×2 = **60 km** from A's side.

**Q8:** Convert 90 km/h = 25 m/s. Distance = 500m. Time = 500/25 = **20 seconds**

**Q9:**
Distance = S₁×S₂×(T₁+T₂)/(S₂-S₁) = 3×4×30/(4-3) = **360 min worth**...
Correct: Distance = 3×4×(20+10)/(4-3) = 12×30 = 360/... 

Let me use the clear method:
Let D = distance, correct time = t hours
3(t + 20/60) = D → 3t + 1 = D
4(t - 10/60) = D → 4t - 2/3 = D
3t + 1 = 4t - 2/3 → t = 1 + 2/3 = **5/3 hours = 100 minutes**
D = 3×(5/3 + 1/3) = 3×2 = **6 km**

**Q10:** B = (15+9)/2 = **12 km/h**. S = (15-9)/2 = **3 km/h**

**Q11:** Head start time = 15 min = 0.25h. Distance head start = 8 × 0.25 = 2 km.
Relative speed = 12 - 8 = 4 km/h. Time = 2/4 = **0.5 hours = 30 minutes** after police starts.

**Q12:** Relative speed (opposite) = 5+3 = 8 m/s. Meetings/minute = 8×60/600 = 0.8/min.
In 10 min: 0.8 × 10 = **8 times**

## 🔴 LEVEL 3 — Hard (Exam Level)

**Q13.** Train A (150m, 66 km/h) and Train B (speed unknown) run opposite. They cross in 12 sec. Train A crosses a 180m platform in 21 sec. Find Train B's length and speed.

**Q14.** A boat takes 4 hours to go upstream and 2 hours downstream for same distance. If stream speed is 6 km/h, find boat speed.

**Q15.** A person goes from A to B at 10 km/h and returns. He then goes again at 15 km/h and returns. What is the average speed for the whole journey?

**Q16.** Two trains start simultaneously from stations P and Q toward each other. After crossing, Train A takes 4 hours and Train B takes 9 hours to reach the other station. Find ratio of their speeds.

**Q17.** A walks at 4 km/h, rests 30 min, walks again at 5 km/h, rests 30 min. Total distance 16 km, total time 4 hours. Find time walking at each speed.

**Q18.** A clock gains 5 minutes every hour. If set correctly at 8 AM, what time does it show at 8 PM?

---

### 🔴 Level 3 Answers

**Q13:**
Train A speed: From platform: (150+180)/21 = 330/21 = 110/7 m/s

Wait, convert: 66 km/h = 66×5/18 = 55/3 m/s ≈ 18.33 m/s
Platform crossing: 150 + 180 = 330m in 21 sec → Speed = 330/21 ≈ 15.71 m/s

Hmm, inconsistent — meaning the 21 sec already gives us speed:
Speed_A = 330/21 = 110/7 m/s (the 66 km/h was for context of crossing)

For crossing two trains:
Relative speed × 12 = 150 + L_B
Relative speed = (speed_A + speed_B)

Using speed_A = 110/7:
Find speed_B: 66 km/h = 110/6 m/s. Let speed_B = v.
(110/6 + v) × 12 = 150 + L_B ... (more info needed for unique solution)

**Simplified version:** Speed_A = 330/21 m/s.
If Speed_B = 22 m/s (given/assumed): Relative = 110/7+22 = 264/7. 264/7 × 12 = 3168/7 = 452.57m. L_B = 452.57-150 = **302.57m**

**Q14:** Let distance = d.
d/(B-6) = 4h → d = 4(B-6)
d/(B+6) = 2h → d = 2(B+6)
4(B-6) = 2(B+6)
4B-24 = 2B+12
2B = 36 → **B = 18 km/h** ✅

**Q15:** Round trip 1 avg: 2×10×10/(10+10) = **10 km/h**
Round trip 2 avg: 2×15×15/(15+15) = **15 km/h**
Total avg (equal distances each round trip): 2×10×15/(10+15) = 300/25 = **12 km/h** ✅

**Q16:** After crossing, A covers what B already covered, and B covers what A already covered.
Time_A_after / Time_B_after = (Speed_B/Speed_A)²  ← Classic result!
4/9 = (B/A)² → B/A = 2/3 → **Speed_A : Speed_B = 3 : 2** ✅

**Q17:** Let time at 4 km/h = t₁ hours, at 5 km/h = t₂ hours
Rest time = 30+30 min = 1 hour
t₁ + t₂ + 1 = 4 → t₁ + t₂ = 3
4t₁ + 5t₂ = 16
From first: t₁ = 3 - t₂
4(3-t₂) + 5t₂ = 16 → 12 - 4t₂ + 5t₂ = 16 → t₂ = 4... but t₁+t₂=3, so t₂≤3.
Recheck: 4t₁+5t₂=16, t₁+t₂=3 → Subtract: t₂=16-12=4. Contradiction → **impossible as given** (check the problem parameters)

Corrected: If total = 4.5 hours: rest = 1h, walking = 3.5h. 4t₁+5t₂=16, t₁+t₂=3.5 → t₂=2h, t₁=1.5h ✅

**Q18:** Gains 5 min per hour. 8 AM to 8 PM = 12 real hours.
Total gain = 12 × 5 = 60 minutes.
Clock shows: 8 PM + 60 min = **9 PM** ✅

## 🏆 LEVEL 4 — Competition Level

**Q19.** Two cyclists A and B start at the same time from P and Q (120 km apart) toward each other. A's speed = 30 km/h. They first meet at point M. After meeting, A continues to Q, stays 1 hour, returns. B also continues to P, stays 40 min, returns. When and where do they meet next?

**Q20.** A train overtakes two persons walking in the same direction at 4 km/h and 5 km/h and passes them in 12 and 15 seconds respectively. Find train length and speed.

---

### 🏆 Level 4 Answers

**Q20:**
Let train speed = v m/s, length = L m
Convert walkers: 4 km/h = 10/9 m/s, 5 km/h = 25/18 m/s

L = (v - 10/9) × 12 = (v - 25/18) × 15
12v - 120/9 = 15v - 375/18
12v - 40/3 = 15v - 125/6
125/6 - 40/3 = 3v
125/6 - 80/6 = 3v
45/6 = 3v → v = 45/18 = 2.5 m/s = **9 km/h**
L = (2.5 - 10/9) × 12 = (22.5/9 - 10/9) × 12 = (12.5/9) × 12 = 150/9 ≈ **16.67 m**

> (Problem likely expects nicer numbers — but the method is exactly this! ✅)
