## 🧒 Explain It Like You're 6

Imagine you're walking to school.

- **Distance** = how far school is (say, 2 km away)
- **Time** = how long it takes you (say, 30 minutes)
- **Speed** = how *fast* you walked

> 🏃 Speed tells you: **"How much distance do you cover in ONE unit of time?"**

If you walk **2 km in 1 hour**, your speed = **2 km per hour (km/h)**.

---

## 🌍 Real World Connections

| Situation | Distance | Time | Speed |
|-----------|----------|------|-------|
| Car on highway | 100 km | 2 hours | 50 km/h |
| Bolt running 100m | 100 m | 9.58 sec | ~10.4 m/s |
| Light traveling | 300,000 km | 1 second | 3,00,000 km/s |
| You walking | 1 km | 15 min | 4 km/h |

---

## 🧠 The Core Relationship

These three things are always connected:

```
Speed × Time = Distance
```

**Think of it like this:**

> If you drive at 60 km/h for 2 hours, you've covered:
> 60 × 2 = **120 km**

That's it. That's the whole game. Everything else in TSD is just this formula in disguise!

---

## 💡 Feynman Insight #1 — The "Per" Word

Whenever you see **"per"** — think DIVISION.

- km **per** hour = km ÷ hour
- meters **per** second = meters ÷ second
- ₹100 **per** kg = ₹100 ÷ kg

> **Speed = Distance ÷ Time** (Distance PER unit of Time)

---

## 💡 Feynman Insight #2 — The Ant Story

Imagine an ant walking on a ruler.

- The ruler = the **distance** (road)
- The ant's pace = the **speed**
- The clock ticking = the **time**

If the ant moves faster → covers more ruler marks in same time → higher speed ✅

If the road is longer → ant needs more time at same pace ✅

If you give the ant more time → it covers more distance ✅

**These three always dance together. Change one, the others respond.**

---

## 🔑 Key Terms (Don't Mix These Up!)

| Term | Meaning | Unit |
|------|---------|------|
| **Distance** | Total path covered | km, m, miles |
| **Displacement** | Straight-line from start to end | km, m |
| **Speed** | Distance ÷ Time | km/h, m/s |
| **Velocity** | Speed with direction | km/h, m/s |
| **Time** | Duration of travel | hours, minutes, seconds |

> 💬 For aptitude exams: **use Speed and Distance** (direction doesn't matter unless stated)

## ✅ Self-Test (Before Moving On)

1. A car covers 150 km in 3 hours. What is its speed?
2. A cyclist rides at 20 km/h for 45 minutes. How far does she go?
3. A train covers 360 km at 90 km/h. How long does it take?

> **Answers:** 1) 50 km/h &nbsp; 2) 15 km &nbsp; 3) 4 hours
