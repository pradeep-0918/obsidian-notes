## 🧒 Why Does This Matter?

Imagine a question says: *"A car moves at 72 km/h. What is this in m/s?"*

Most students panic. But once you learn ONE trick, you'll solve this in **under 3 seconds** — without a calculator!

---

## 🎯 The Two Conversions You Must Master

### Conversion 1: km/h → m/s

$$\text{m/s} = \text{km/h} \times \frac{5}{18}$$

> **Why 5/18?**
> 1 km = 1000 m
> 1 hour = 3600 seconds
> So: km/h × (1000/3600) = km/h × **5/18** ✅

---

### Conversion 2: m/s → km/h

$$\text{km/h} = \text{m/s} \times \frac{18}{5}$$

> Just flip it! Multiply by 18/5 instead.

---

## 🧠 Memory Trick — "To Slow Down, Use 5/18"

- Going from **big unit to small unit** (km/h → m/s) → multiply by **5/18** (number gets smaller ✓)
- Going from **small unit to big unit** (m/s → km/h) → multiply by **18/5** (number gets bigger ✓)

> 🎯 **Check yourself:** 18 m/s × 18/5 = 64.8 km/h (yes, faster!) ✅

## 🧪 Solved Examples

### Example 1
> Convert 90 km/h to m/s.

90 × 5/18 = **25 m/s** ✅ (direct from table!)

---

### Example 2
> Convert 15 m/s to km/h.

15 × 18/5 = 15 × 3.6 = **54 km/h** ✅

---

### Example 3
> A train runs at 20 m/s. How many km does it cover in 3 hours?

First: 20 m/s × 18/5 = **72 km/h**
Then: D = 72 × 3 = **216 km** ✅

*OR keep in m/s:*
D = 20 m/s × (3 × 3600 sec) = 20 × 10800 = 2,16,000 m = **216 km** ✅

---

### Example 4 (Exam Favorite!)
> A man covers 1.5 km in 15 minutes. Speed in km/h?

Distance = 1.5 km
Time = 15 min = 15/60 hours = 1/4 hour

Speed = 1.5 ÷ (1/4) = 1.5 × 4 = **6 km/h** ✅

---

## 🚨 Common Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| 72 km/h × 18/5 | 72 km/h × 5/18 (to get m/s) |
| 30 min = 0.3 hours | 30 min = 0.5 hours (= 30/60) |
| Speed in km/h, Time in minutes → D in km | Convert time to hours first! |

---

## 💡 The 3.6 Shortcut

> m/s → km/h: **multiply by 3.6** (same as 18/5)

This is the easiest way to remember it. 1 m/s = 3.6 km/h.

- 10 m/s = 36 km/h
- 25 m/s = 90 km/h
- 100 m/s = 360 km/h
