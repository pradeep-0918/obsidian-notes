## 🎩 The One Tool That Solves Everything

Meet the **DST Triangle** — the most powerful tool in TSD:

```
        [ D ]
       /     \
    [ S ]×[ T ]
```

**How to use it:**

> Cover the thing you want to find. What's left is how to calculate it.

| Want to find | Cover | Formula |
|---|---|---|
| **Distance (D)** | Cover D | S × T |
| **Speed (S)** | Cover S | D ÷ T |
| **Time (T)** | Cover T | D ÷ S |

---

## 🖐️ The Hand Trick

Draw this on your palm right now:

```
     D
   -----
   S | T
```

- Want **D**? → Multiply S × T
- Want **S**? → Divide D ÷ T  
- Want **T**? → Divide D ÷ S

> 🎯 **Pro Tip:** Write this on scratch paper in EVERY exam. It takes 3 seconds and saves minutes of thinking.

---

## 📖 The Three Formulas (Memorize These Cold)

### Formula 1: Finding Distance
$$D = S \times T$$

> "I drove at 80 km/h for 3 hours → 80 × 3 = **240 km**"

---

### Formula 2: Finding Speed
$$S = \frac{D}{T}$$

> "I covered 120 km in 2 hours → 120 ÷ 2 = **60 km/h**"

---

### Formula 3: Finding Time
$$T = \frac{D}{S}$$

> "I need to cover 150 km at 50 km/h → 150 ÷ 50 = **3 hours**"

---

## 🧪 Let's Solve 5 Problems Together

### Problem 1 (Easy — Finding Distance)
> A bike runs at 60 km/h for 2.5 hours. How far?

**Step 1:** Identify → S = 60, T = 2.5, D = ?
**Step 2:** D = S × T = 60 × 2.5 = **150 km** ✅

---

### Problem 2 (Easy — Finding Speed)
> A train covers 480 km in 6 hours. What's its speed?

S = D ÷ T = 480 ÷ 6 = **80 km/h** ✅

---

### Problem 3 (Easy — Finding Time)
> A car needs to go 300 km at 75 km/h. How long?

T = D ÷ S = 300 ÷ 75 = **4 hours** ✅

---

### Problem 4 (Medium — Mixed Units!)
> A person walks 3 km in 45 minutes. Speed in km/h?

**Watch out!** Time is in minutes, speed needs to be in km/h (hours).

Convert: 45 minutes = 45/60 hours = **0.75 hours**

S = 3 ÷ 0.75 = **4 km/h** ✅

> 🚨 **Most Common Mistake:** Mixing up units! Always check that D, S, T use the same unit of time.

---

### Problem 5 (Tricky — Change in Speed)
> A car first covers 100 km at 50 km/h, then 100 km at 100 km/h. Total time?

Time 1 = 100 ÷ 50 = 2 hours
Time 2 = 100 ÷ 100 = 1 hour
**Total Time = 2 + 1 = 3 hours** ✅

> Note: Total Distance = 200 km, Total Time = 3 hours
> Average Speed = 200 ÷ 3 = 66.67 km/h ← NOT 75! (More in [[04 - Average Speed (The Tricky One)]])

## 🔁 The "Always True" Relationships

```
D = S × T       (Golden Rule)
S = D / T       (Speed formula)
T = D / S       (Time formula)

If S doubles → T halves (D stays same)  ← Inverse relationship!
If S doubles → D doubles (T stays same) ← Direct relationship!
```

---

## ⚡ Speed Trick: Ratio Shortcut

When distance is **fixed**:
> Speed and Time are **inversely proportional** (S₁ × T₁ = S₂ × T₂)

**Example:** A person usually takes 3 hours. If they increase speed by 50%, new time?
- New speed = 1.5× → New time = 1/1.5 × 3 = **2 hours** 

No need to find actual speed or distance! ✅
