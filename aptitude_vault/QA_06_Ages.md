# 👴 Ages
> Part of [[01_Quant_Index]] | 🔙 [[aptitude_vault/00_Master_Index]]

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐ Easy-Medium | **Exam Weight:** Medium

---

## 🧠 Concept

Age problems use algebra. Key insight: **age differences never change.**
> If A is 10 years older than B today, A will ALWAYS be 10 years older.

**x years ago:** Current age − x
**x years hence:** Current age + x

---

## 📌 Key Approach

1. Let present age = variable (e.g., A = a, B = b)
2. Write equations based on given conditions
3. Solve the system of equations

### Standard Setup
| Condition | Equation |
|---|---|
| Sum of ages = S | a + b = S |
| Ratio now = m:n | a/b = m/n |
| Ratio x years ago = p:q | (a-x)/(b-x) = p/q |
| A is k years older than B | a = b + k |

---

## 🔥 Key Patterns

**Pattern 1: Ratio changes over time**
> "Ratio now is 2:3. After 5 years, ratio is 3:4."
- a/b = 2/3 → a = 2k, b = 3k
- (2k+5)/(3k+5) = 3/4 → 8k+20 = 9k+15 → k = 5
- Ages: 10, 15

**Pattern 2: n years ago/hence + current sum**
> "Sum of ages = 70. 10 years ago, A was twice B. Find ages."
- a + b = 70; (a-10) = 2(b-10)
- a = 2b-10; 2b-10+b=70; 3b=80; b≈26.67

**Pattern 3: Three people**
> "Sum of A, B, C = 90. 10 years ago, A was twice B, B was twice C."
- 10 years ago: C=c, B=2c, A=4c
- Now: C=c+10, B=2c+10, A=4c+10
- Sum: 7c+30=90; 7c=60; c≈8.57

---

## 💡 Shortcuts

- **Age difference is constant** — use it to check answers
- Set up ratio as multiples: a:b = 3:4 → a=3k, b=4k

---

## 🧩 Practice Questions

### Easy
1. A is 30, B is 20. Ratio? → **3:2**
2. A is twice B. B is 15. A? → **30**
3. Sum of A and B = 50. A = 20. B? → **30**

### Medium
4. Ratio A:B = 2:3. In 5 years = 3:4. Ages? → **10, 15**
5. 10 years ago A was twice B. Now A=30. B now? → A-10 = 2(B-10); 20=2B-20; B = **20**

### Hard
6. Five years ago A was twice B. Five years hence A is 1.5× B. Ages?
   > (A-5)=2(B-5); (A+5)=1.5(B+5)
   > A-5=2B-10; A=2B-5
   > 2B-5+5=1.5(B+5); 2B=1.5B+7.5; 0.5B=7.5; B=15; A=25
